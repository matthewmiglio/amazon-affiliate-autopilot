# Niche: Luxe Lifestyle for Less

**Tagline candidates:** "Luxe lifestyle for less", "Affordable luxury daily", "Everyday luxe finds"

**Hero category:** Amazon Luxury Beauty (10% commission)
**Filler category:** Fashion / Jewelry / Watches / Luggage (4% commission)

---

## Positioning

Female-targeted Shorts channel showcasing **Amazon-available luxury beauty + elevated fashion** at price points that feel attainable. The promise to the viewer: "the same aesthetic as a luxury influencer's daily routine, sourced from products you can one-click on Amazon right now."

The on-screen presence is an **AI-generated attractive woman** demonstrating the products — applying serum, swiping lipstick, modelling jewelry, styling an OOTD. The visual style is Pinterest-aesthetic, soft warm lighting, marble/gold/cream colour palette.

---

## Why this niche

**Commission math** — Luxury Beauty at 10% pays 3.3× more per sale than standard beauty (3%) and 10× more than Health/grocery (1%). On a single $80 perfume conversion that's $8, vs $2.40 in the standard category. AOV in this category sits at $40–150.

**Cart-cookie multiplier** — Amazon's 24-hour cookie means a click on a $90 serum link can pay 10% on the serum + 4% on the dress + 4% on the sunglasses if the viewer cart-buys within the window. Hero category as the entry point + Fashion as filler stacks earnings across one click.

**Format-content fit** — Luxury beauty *requires demonstration* (the application, the texture, the glow). Without a person on-screen, it's just bottle photography, which loses to every channel doing it with humans. The AI-attractive-woman approach is the differentiator.

**Demographic alignment** — Women 18–35, Pinterest/IG-aesthetic crowd, dupe culture overlap, high purchase intent on visual recommendations.

---

## Target audience

- **Primary:** Women 22–32, college-educated, beauty-aware, mid-income aspirational. Buys at Sephora occasionally, would buy more if convenient.
- **Secondary:** Women 18–24 just discovering luxury beauty, learning what's worth it.
- **Buying mindset:** "Show me what's worth it. Show me what looks expensive but isn't out of reach."

## Product types (in order of priority)

### Hero products (Luxury Beauty — 10%)
1. **Premium fragrance** — designer perfumes ($60–200). Highest AOV, strong impulse-buy energy.
2. **Luxury skincare** — serums, eye creams, masks (La Mer, Augustinus Bader, Drunk Elephant via Luxury Stores). $40–250.
3. **High-end lip products** — Charlotte Tilbury, Tom Ford, Dior. $40–60.
4. **Premium hair tools** — Dyson Airwrap, T3 (verify category eligibility per-product). $200–600 — huge AOV when it converts.

### Filler products (Fashion family — 4%)
5. **Dainty / layered jewelry** — gold-plated necklaces, earrings ($20–80).
6. **Watches** — minimalist designer watches ($80–300).
7. **Handbags** — affordable-luxury leather, crossbody styles ($60–200).
8. **Sunglasses** — designer + designer-look ($30–150).
9. **Outfit pieces** — slip dresses, cashmere, blazers featured in OOTD shots ($40–150).

### Avoid
- Standard drugstore beauty (only 3%, dilutes the "luxe" positioning).
- Skincare devices that route to Health & Personal Care (1%).
- Wellness / supplements (1%).
- Anything that requires explicit health/efficacy claims (FTC + Amazon TOS minefield).

## Content formats

Mix across the launch batch and ongoing posting:

1. **"GRWM with Amazon luxury finds"** — get-ready-with-me, 4–6 products in sequence, soft narration or trending audio. Hero format.
2. **"$X dupe vs $XXX original"** — Tom Ford perfume vs Amazon find, side-by-side. Search-driven, dupe culture catnip.
3. **"What's in my Amazon cart this week"** — multi-product montage, mostly luxury beauty + 1–2 fashion items.
4. **"This Amazon fragrance gets compliments every time"** — single-product spotlight, single hero shot, soft CTA.
5. **"Luxe vanity restock"** — aesthetic flat-lay reveal, ASMR-adjacent.
6. **"OOTD with [item] from Amazon"** — fashion-led, jewelry/handbag accents.

Every Short has the AI woman either fully on-screen or partial (hands applying, neck wearing necklace, side profile spritzing perfume). No faceless montages — that's what every other Amazon channel does and we're explicitly rejecting that approach.

## Commission math (rough projection)

Assume mature channel: 5 Shorts/week → 1 conversion per 5,000 views → average 50,000 views/week → ~10 conversions/week.

| Mix | Avg sale | Rate | Per-conversion | 10/wk | Monthly |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 70% Luxury Beauty | $60 | 10% | $6.00 | $42 | $168 |
| 20% Fashion | $50 | 4% | $2.00 | $4 | $16 |
| 10% Jewelry/watches | $40 | 4% | $1.60 | $1.60 | $6.40 |
| **Total** | | | | **~$48/wk** | **~$190/mo** |

Real numbers depend heavily on conversion rate (this assumes 0.02% click-to-purchase, which is conservative). Doubling the conversion rate or scaling to 2 Shorts/day puts you at $400+/mo, which is the realistic 6-month target.

The conversion-rate ceiling is what to optimize for: better hooks, better product picks, better thumbnail/title, more demonstration in the first 2 seconds. Volume is fixed by posting cadence; conversion rate is the lever.

## Risks & guardrails

1. **AI face quality** — luxury beauty leans heavily on close-up faces. If the model output is uncanny, viewers bounce instantly. Test the model on close-up makeup application *before* committing 10 Shorts to it. If quality is borderline, lean toward jewelry/fashion (less close-face dependent).

2. **Luxury Beauty product availability** — not every "luxury" brand on Amazon is in the Luxury Stores program. The 10% rate only applies to products Amazon flags as Luxury Stores Beauty. Verify per-product before featuring (check the product page for the "Luxury Stores" badge, or use the Associates linking tool — the rate it shows is the rate you'll get).

3. **No price claims** — never say "only $40" in audio/video; prices change. Say "under $50" or "affordable luxury" (already a rule per `rules.md`).

4. **No counterfeit / grey-market call-outs** — Amazon's luxury beauty selection is sometimes flagged for authenticity issues. Don't link to anything you'd hesitate to recommend; refunds chargeback against your earnings.

5. **FTC compliance unchanged** — disclosure on every Short, in description + on-screen + spoken. See `rules.md`.

6. **Saturation** — the "Amazon luxury finds" angle is *less* saturated than general beauty but still competitive. The differentiator is content quality (AI woman + aesthetic palette), not the niche claim itself.
