# Channel Startup: Female-Targeted Niche on YouTube Shorts

Goal: launch a Shorts channel marketed to women, post 8–10 Shorts to build a base, then apply to Amazon Associates with the channel URL.

---

## Niche suggestions (ranked by AI-video friendliness + affiliate fit)

Ranked by: how easy it is to make compelling 15s AI video without owning the product, average order value, conversion intent, and saturation level.

### S-tier (best fit)

**1. "Amazon beauty finds under $20"** — makeup, skincare, hair tools
- Why it works: high impulse buy, women search "Amazon must-haves" daily, evergreen
- Visual: AI-generated product close-ups, swatches, before/after morphs
- AOV: $10–25, but volume is enormous
- Saturation: HIGH, but algorithm still pushes new accounts in this niche
- Channel name idea: `@beautyfindsdaily`, `@under20beauty`, `@amazonglowups`

**2. "Aesthetic room / desk / vanity decor"** — fairy lights, mirrors, organizers, candles, prints
- Why it works: Pinterest-aesthetic crowd, very visual, women 16–30 dominate this audience
- Visual: AI-generated room flythroughs, cozy ambient scenes, "shelf transformation"
- AOV: $15–60
- Saturation: MEDIUM
- Channel name idea: `@cozyfindshop`, `@vanityfinds`, `@aestheticamzn`

**3. "Self-care / nighttime routine essentials"** — silk pillowcases, face masks, teas, journals, candles
- Why it works: lifestyle hook is stronger than product hook; viewer imagines themselves using it
- Visual: AI-generated cozy scenes (warm lighting, hands holding tea, etc.)
- AOV: $20–40
- Saturation: MEDIUM
- Channel name idea: `@cozyselfcare`, `@nightroutinefinds`

### A-tier (solid)

**4. "Jewelry under $30 that looks expensive"** — gold-plated, dainty, layered necklaces, earrings
- Why it works: dupe culture is huge, viewers screenshot to send to friends
- Visual: AI close-ups, neck/wrist shots (no real face needed)
- AOV: $15–30
- Saturation: HIGH but sustainable
- Channel name idea: `@daintydupes`, `@goldfindshop`

**5. "Makeup dupes for [luxury brand]"** — Charlotte Tilbury dupes, Dior dupes
- Why it works: search-driven, high intent ("I want that thing but cheaper")
- Visual: side-by-side AI product comparisons
- AOV: $10–20
- Saturation: HIGH
- Channel name idea: `@dupedeals`, `@luxedupes`

**6. "Energy drink reviews / aesthetic"** — Alani Nu, Celsius, Bloom
- Why it works: cult-following brands, women 18–28 are the core demographic, Alani Nu especially
- Visual: AI-rendered cans in aesthetic settings (gym bag, desk, car cup holder)
- AOV: $15–30 per multi-pack
- Saturation: LOW–MEDIUM (under-served compared to general beauty)
- Channel name idea: `@cancrush`, `@dailyenergyfinds`
- ⚠️ Caveat: caffeine/health claims need to stay vague — don't say "this energy drink will help you lose weight"

### B-tier (workable but harder)

**7. "Art supplies / journaling aesthetic"** — gouache sets, washi tape, sketchbooks, brush pens
- Why it works: extremely loyal niche audience, repeat purchasers
- Visual: AI-rendered flat-lay supplies, hands drawing
- AOV: $15–40
- Saturation: LOW
- Downside: smaller audience, slower channel growth

**8. "Nail art / press-on nails finds"**
- Why it works: very visual, dupe culture, micro-trends weekly
- Visual: AI-rendered hands with nail close-ups (this is where AI sometimes glitches — fingers)
- AOV: $10–20
- Saturation: MEDIUM
- Caveat: AI hands are notoriously bad. May need to avoid.

---

## My recommendation

**Start with "Aesthetic home / vanity decor finds"** (S-tier #2).

Reasons specific to your situation:
- AI video generation handles **objects in scenes** much better than **faces, hands, or makeup application**. A candle on a nightstand is easy. A face applying eyeliner is hard.
- The audience (women 18–35, Pinterest-aesthetic) has **high purchase intent** and **trusts visual recommendations**.
- Saturation is medium — beauty is brutal, decor is breathable.
- Products are **photogenic without needing demonstration** (a candle just needs to look cozy; mascara needs to be applied to mean anything).
- Average order value is higher than makeup ($15–60 vs $10–20).

Backup pick: **energy drinks** if you want lower saturation and don't mind the slightly harder content angle.

## Channel setup checklist

### Before posting anything

- [ ] **Pick handle** (e.g. `@cozyfindshop`) — check availability on YouTube + TikTok + IG (claim all even if you only post to YT)
- [ ] **Channel name** (display name can differ from handle)
- [ ] **Profile pic** — simple, recognizable at 32px. AI-generated logo or aesthetic icon.
- [ ] **Banner** — 2048×1152, niche-clear ("Daily aesthetic Amazon finds 🕯️")
- [ ] **About section** — 2 sentences. Include "As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases."
- [ ] **Links section** — placeholder for now (will be Amazon storefront URL once approved)
- [ ] **Channel keywords** — set in Studio → Settings → Channel → Basic info

### First-batch content (8–10 Shorts before applying)

The applying-for-Associates batch should look like a *real channel*, not a desperate launch. Mix:

1. **3–4 "single product spotlight"** — hero a single item, hook → product → soft CTA
2. **2–3 "top 5 finds under $X"** — multi-product montage (these get more views)
3. **1–2 "aesthetic POV / lifestyle"** — no specific product, just the vibe (sets the channel's tone)
4. **1 "introducing the channel"** — short, says what you post, builds the "about" identity

For all of these, the description can say "Links coming soon ✨" or "Apply via DM" — **do NOT use Amazon links yet.** You don't have an account. Once approved, edit the descriptions in.

### Format spec (every Short)

- **Resolution:** 1080×1920, 30fps
- **Length:** 15–25s sweet spot
- **Audio:** YouTube free library trending audio (check Studio → Audio Library, sort by Recent)
- **Captions:** Burned in, large, center-bottom
- **First frame:** Hook text overlay
- **Last frame:** Loop-friendly OR soft CTA ("Follow for more")

### Posting schedule for the launch batch

- Days 1–2: 1 Short each
- Days 3–7: 1 Short/day
- Day 8: review which performed best, lean into that style
- Day 9–10: post final 2 Shorts
- **Day 10–11: apply to Amazon Associates** with channel URL

### Apply-day prep

- Channel must be **public**
- About section must mention the niche clearly
- At least 1 Short must have **>100 views** (algorithmic proof of life)
- Application questions: pick "lifestyle / shopping" category, mention Shorts as primary traffic, list ~5–10 product types you'll feature

---

## What "good base" means before applying

Amazon's reviewer is checking: *is this a real channel a real person would trust?*

Pass: 8+ videos, consistent niche, branded pfp/banner, clear about section, public, posts within last 14 days.

Fail: empty channel, generic name, no banner, mixed-niche videos, last upload was a month ago.

You don't need subscribers. You don't need viral views. You need to look **legitimate and on-topic**.

---

## After approval (week 3+)

- Edit existing Shorts' descriptions to add affiliate links + disclosure
- Set up Amazon storefront (free, takes 10 min) and link it in channel "Links"
- Switch to 2 Shorts/day cadence
- Track in `data/`: which products convert, which video styles convert, time-of-day performance
- After 30 days post-approval, evaluate: hit 3 qualifying sales? If no, the channel-niche-product fit is wrong, not the channel itself
